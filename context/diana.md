## SERVICE BRIEF (what you're selling)

**Offer:** Expat offshore financial planning. You introduce internationally-based professionals
to the firm and book them a **complimentary 30–45 min financial health check** with a senior
consultant/adviser.

**Target:** Expats — e.g. British expats, South Africans, oil-and-gas professionals — living
abroad whose employer doesn't help with offshore planning.

**The three hooks (the needs you surface):**
1. Retirement planning / saving for children
2. Protecting money from inflation and unnecessary global tax
3. Digital assets

**Goal of the call:** Secure the meeting. **Not** to give advice, not to pitch products, not to
close a sale on the phone. The meeting is the win.

---

## CALL MAP (the structure to follow — paraphrased from the firm script)

1. **Introduction** — greet, name the firm, one-line on what you do and who you help, ask if
   they know the company. Light, quick, friendly.
2. **Fact-find** — the heart of it: **mostly questions.** How long in country? Settled in?
   Managing money yourself or with an adviser? Saving here or sending home? Here with family?
   Where from originally? Build rapport throughout. Surface which of the three hooks matters to them.
3. **Solution** — suggest the complimentary health check with a named senior consultant, assume
   the meeting, agree time/format, confirm details, ask what else they'd want from it. Close warmly.

## OBJECTIONS (intent · try · pivot · principles)
*Drafts — refine with firm-approved wording. Every response should re-open with a QUESTION, not a pitch.*

**1. Not interested**
- *Intent:* reflex brush-off before they know what it is — not a real no.
- *Try:* "That's completely fair — we've only just spoken. Can I ask, with your savings out here, are you managing things yourself at the moment or working with someone?"
- *Pivot:* from "not interested" → a no-stakes question about their current setup.
- *Principles:* don't argue; lower the stakes; swap the pitch for curiosity; get them talking about themselves.

**2. Send me an email**
- *Intent:* polite deferral to end the call.
- *Try:* "Happy to — and so I send something actually relevant rather than generic, can I ask: is it retirement, tax efficiency, or growth you'd most want it to cover?"
- *Pivot:* from "email me" → a qualifying question that personalises and re-engages.
- *Principles:* don't refuse; use the email to earn one more question; relevance needs a conversation.

**3. Too busy**
- *Intent:* time pressure — could be genuine or a brush-off.
- *Try:* "Completely understand, I'll be quick. Is now just bad timing, or is financial planning something that's been on the back burner generally?"
- *Pivot:* from "no time" → separate real timing from disinterest; book later if it's timing.
- *Principles:* honour their time; one question; offer to book rather than push now.

**4. I have an adviser**
- *Intent:* feels covered — possibly genuine, possibly a shield.
- *Try:* "Good to hear — more than a lot of expats have. Can I ask, are they out here and specialised in expat planning, or back home?"
- *Pivot:* from "I'm covered" → a question that surfaces whether their adviser actually fits the expat situation.
- *Principles:* never knock the incumbent; differentiate on expat-specialism; let a question reveal the gap.

**5. Where did you get my details**  ⚠️ HONESTY-CRITICAL
- *Intent:* privacy concern / mild suspicion — needs a straight answer to rebuild trust.
- *Try:* "Fair question — [YOUR ACTUAL LEAD SOURCE, stated truthfully]. I'm not here to be pushy, I just introduce expats to what we do. Can I ask, are you getting any guidance on the offshore side at the moment?"
- *Pivot:* suspicion → transparency → a low-pressure question.
- *Principles:* **tell the truth about the source — never deflect or invent.** Transparency defuses; then re-open gently. (Fill in your real source; DIANA must never coach a made-up answer here.)

**6. Bad experience before**
- *Intent:* real grievance, emotionally loaded — an opening to listen.
- *Try:* "I'm sorry to hear that — you're not the first to say it. Do you mind me asking what happened? It helps me understand what you'd want done differently."
- *Pivot:* "burned before" → let them tell the story; listening = rapport + fact-find.
- *Principles:* don't defend the industry; listen; their story is the fact-find; differentiate by being different right now.

**7. No money / nothing to invest**
- *Intent:* assumes it's about a lump sum — possibly genuine, possibly reflex.
- *Try:* "Totally fine — a lot of what we do isn't about having a pot ready to invest, it's making the most of your tax-free income while you're out here. Can I ask, are you able to put anything aside monthly at the moment?"
- *Pivot:* "no lump sum" → regular savings / making the most of expat income.
- *Principles:* don't accept the framing; broaden the value; ask about monthly capacity, not wealth.

**8. Called before**
- *Intent:* mild irritation / been-here-before — don't repeat, do differentiate.
- *Try:* "Apologies if we've crossed paths before. Did you ever end up having a proper conversation with someone, or did it not go further at the time?"
- *Pivot:* "heard it before" → find the unfinished thread / what stalled it.
- *Principles:* acknowledge without grovelling; a question finds whether it's a real no or just never completed.

## EVALUATION RUBRIC (how DIANA scores a roleplay)

The skill being trained is **question-led, let-them-talk selling.** After a roleplay, score each:

1. **Talk ratio** — did the *prospect* talk more than you? (Target: them > you. Pitching = you dominate = mark down.)
2. **Open questions** — did you ask open, exploratory questions rather than pitch or ask closed yes/nos?
3. **Rapport** — did you acknowledge, empathise, and react to what they said (not just run a script)?
4. **Need-led** — did you surface one of the three hooks through *questions*, letting them name the need, rather than telling them what they need?
5. **Objection handling** — did you meet objections with acknowledgement + a question, instead of arguing or jumping to a pitch?
6. **Stayed in lane** — did you resist giving advice or pitching product on the call? (The goal is the meeting.)
7. **Secured the next step** — did you move toward booking the health check?

**Feedback style:** warm and specific. Lead with what worked, name the single highest-leverage fix
(usually talk ratio or leading with a question), quote one good line they used and one to improve.
Never harsh.

## DIFFICULTY
- `warm` — receptive prospect, light objections.
- `neutral` (default) — realistic, one or two objections, needs real questions to open up.
- `tough` — guarded, multiple objections, only opens up if you genuinely ask and listen.
